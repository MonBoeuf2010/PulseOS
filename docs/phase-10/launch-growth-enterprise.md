# Phase 10 — Launch, Growth & Enterprise Readiness

> Authored from four seats: **YC Partner** (find PMF, do things that don't scale), **Sequoia Partner** (market, moat, durable economics), **Growth Lead** (PLG loops), **Enterprise Sales Lead** (land-and-expand). The connective tissue is Phase 1's discipline: win the wedge, gate the rest.

---

## 1. Launch strategy

### Pre-launch (private beta)
- **Do things that don't scale:** hand-recruit 50–100 "Maya" operators in **one vertical + one geo** (the founding wedge). Concierge-onboard each; the founders read every briefing and every piece of feedback.
- **Seed the intelligence ourselves** so day-one briefings are genuinely good (no cold-start emptiness). The product must feel magical for one persona before it's wide.
- Instrument the **activation metric** (useful action in session 1) and **WARU** (North Star) from the first user.

### Launch sequence
1. **Private beta** (invite-only, waitlist) → prove H1–H4 (Phase 1 wedge hypotheses). *Do not launch publicly until D30 retention + calibration clear the bar.*
2. **Public launch** in the founding vertical/geo: Product Hunt + founder-led content (the *shareable Council report* is the demo), targeted communities where Maya already lives.
3. **Expand verticals/geos** only after one is retaining and economically sane.

### Anti-pattern to avoid (named)
A splashy "five-ecosystem" launch. We launch *one wedge* that works, not the whole vision. Breadth at launch = shallow everywhere = churn (Phase 1 R-1).

## 2. Product-led growth (PLG) loops

### Loop A — The shareable intelligence loop (primary, viral)
Council report / briefing item has a **share** affordance → recipient sees a beautiful, cited, "so-what/now-what" summary with a soft wall → signs up to get their own personalized version. The *output* is the ad. (Devin/P2 forwarding to colleagues is the engine.)

### Loop B — The value-realized loop (retention→expansion)
Weekly "you saved $X / Yh, caught N opportunities" → reinforces habit → paywalled depth ("see full council / unlock another domain") → Pro upgrade → funds better AI → more value. Self-reinforcing.

### Loop C — The contributor reputation loop (R2, network supply)
Contributors gain portable **reputation + reach** for being right → attracts more contributors → richer intelligence → more readers → higher reputation stakes. Reputation is only valuable inside LifeIQ → lock-in.

### Loop D — Follow/stream loop
Following companies/teams/topics creates a personalized stream → daily reason to return → more follows.

**Onboarding-to-activation** is the top of every loop: <60s to a credible action or the loops never start.

---

## 3. Referral system
- **Give-get** with *intelligence* as currency, not cash: refer → both get a Pro trial / extra council deep-dives / an unlocked opportunity domain (marginal-cost rewards, not discounts that erode price).
- Reputation boost for inviting high-quality contributors (R2).
- Built-in shareables (Loop A) double as organic referral surface.
- Guardrails: anti-abuse (sybil/self-referral detection), capped rewards, fraud monitoring.

## 4. Community growth loops (R2–R3)
- **Seed, don't launch empty.** Founders + paid local experts seed the founding-vertical community with high-signal reports; reputation rewards early accuracy.
- Verification + reputation make quality compound; AI moderation keeps it from degrading into noise/toxicity (the thing that kills Nextdoor/Reddit-likes).
- Geo/vertical expansion only after one community is self-sustaining (DAU + contribution rate + verification precision thresholds).

## 5. Enterprise sales playbook (R4, land-and-expand)

### Land (bottom-up wedge)
- **Meeting Intelligence** is the most independently-sellable wedge into a company that already has Pro users. Land a team via PLG (individual → team workspace).
- Or land via **Corporate Live Rooms** (a company wanting authoritative reach + searchable institutional memory).

### Expand (top-down)
- Bottom-up usage → ROI case to the economic buyer (analytics: modeled value delivered, hours saved, decisions captured) → expand to org-wide + compliance suite.
- **The CISO is the gatekeeper:** lead with Phase 3 security architecture, SOC 2 report, pen-test results, tenancy-isolation proof, DPA, SSO/SCIM, data residency. *Security is the sale.*

### Motion & artifacts
- Sales engineers are security-literate; security review is a *stage*, not a surprise.
- Pilot (segregated/private) → success criteria → annual contract → QBRs driving NRR.
- ICP: data-driven mid-market→enterprise where information advantage is quantifiable (finance, consulting, ops-heavy orgs, sports/media orgs).

## 6. Pricing strategy (operationalizes Phase 1 §8)
- **Free** (wedge funnel, gated AI), **Pro** $15–25/mo (consumer revenue), **Business** $40–80/seat (teams + meetings), **Enterprise** custom $25k–$250k+ (margin).
- **Value-based, tested** (not cost-plus): willingness-to-pay interviews + price experiments. Anchor on value delivered (a single caught opportunity > a year of Pro).
- Protect margin: AI COGS gating, fair-use caps on heavy users, marginal-cost referral rewards.
- Land low (PLG), expand to Business/Enterprise where the durable money is. Open to the truth that **enterprise is the business; consumer is the funnel/brand** (Phase 1/8 honest note).

## 7. Investor pitch outline
1. **Problem:** overload up, decision-time fixed; tools optimize consumption, not action.
2. **Insight/why-now:** LLM cost/quality crossed the line for trustworthy continuous synthesis; trust in raw feeds collapsed.
3. **Product:** the real-time intelligence layer → ranked actions w/ calibrated confidence, evidence, EV, cost-of-inaction. (Demo: the shareable council report.)

6. **Business model:** PLG funnel → Pro → Business → Enterprise margin; sub-linear AI COGS via shared public-signal compute.

8. **Market:** intelligence/decision-support TAM (consumer prosumer + enterprise; Bloomberg/FactSet spend as the ceiling reference).

## 9. Expansion roadmap (post-PMF)
- **Verticals:** founding vertical → adjacent high-value domains (finance→career→business→consumer) as the Opportunity Engine generalizes.
- **Geos:** one→multi, with data residency at Stage 3.
- **Surfaces:** PWA → native mobile (R3) → API/developer platform (R4) → enterprise private deployments.
- **Ecosystems:** dashboard → community → communities → corporate/enterprise, each behind its gate.

## 10. The realistic path: 0 → 1,000,000 (profitability + quality preserved)

| Stage | Users | Focus | How profitability/quality is held |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | private beta (50–100) | concierge, prove H1–H4, seed intel | tiny burn; founders in the loop; quality = hand-tuned |
| **→1k** | one vertical/geo, public | Loop A + content, activation <60s | COGS gating from day 1; precision-first (no cry-wolf) |
| **→10k** | nail retention (D30) | weekly-value loop, referral, Pro launch | free-tier COGS capped; first Pro revenue; calibration bar enforced |
| **→100k** | network (R2): community + reputation + search | Loops C/D, scale infra (replicas, autoscale) | shared public-signal compute keeps COGS sub-linear; SLOs + error budgets protect quality |
| **→1M** | communities (R3) + enterprise (R4) land | enterprise GTM, SOC 2, multi-region | enterprise margin funds scale; gates prevent shipping mediocre ecosystems; reliability → 99.99% |

**Profitability principle:** every stage instruments **cost per MAU** and refuses to scale a loop that isn't economically sane. **Quality principle:** the gates (Phase 12) are hard — no ecosystem ships before its predecessor retains. Growth that outruns trust or unit economics is treated as a bug, not a win.
